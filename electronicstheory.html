sort Study of the V-I characteristics of P-N junction diode

INTRODUCTION

A P-N junction diode's circuit voltage and current are represented by the V I characteristics curve. Under both forward and reverse biases, the curve aids s in calculating the static and dynamic resistances for P-N Junction diodes.

OBJECTIVE

To study the V-I characteristics of a P-N junction diode with the variation of temperature and to determine its static and dynamic resistance.

THEORY

  1. A diode is a device having two terminals in which current flow in only one direction in which the p(anode) and n(cathode).
  2. In an n-type material, the electron is the majority carrier and in p-type the hole is the minority carrier. In p-type material, the hole is the majority carrier.
  3. Biasing refers to the application of an external voltage across the two terminals of the device to extract a response.

Forward bias Condition :

  • A forward bias condition is achieved when positive potential is applied to p-type material and the negative potential is applied to n-type material.
  • The forward bias potential VD will provide pressure to the electrons in n-type materials and holes in the p-type materials which result in the recombination of holes and electrons with ions near the boundary and the width of the depletion region is reduced.
  • As a result, number of immobile ions (acceptor and donor ions) in the depletion region decreases.
  • So, depletion width and junction potential reduce.

Reverse bias Condition :

  • A reversed bias condition is achieved when an external potential is applied across the p-n junction such that the positive terminal is connected to the n-type whereas negative terminal is connected to p-type material.
  • The total number of uncovered positive ions present inside the depletion region of the n-type material will increase because of the greater number of free electrons drained to the positive potential of applied voltage.
  • The number of uncovered negative ions will increase in the p-type material due to the same reason and the depletion region will be increased as the net result.
  • As the reverse biased voltage rises, the strength of the depletion region electric field also rises. The p-n junction depletion zone begins to breakdown if the electric field intensity rises over a critical point, and current starts to flow typically through either the Zener breakdown process or the avalanche breakdown process.
